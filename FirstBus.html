First Bus: Designing the future of travel for millions of passengers.

Overview

First Bus, one of the UK’s largest public transport operators, embarked on a multi-year digital transformation to shift its business model from operational efficiency to aggressive growth. With a target to deliver £80m in additional revenue over 5 years, the business required a complete rethinking of its end-to-end digital service.

I was engaged to define the customer experience vision, support the internal business case, and build the requirements to ensure that future investments in digital products and operational changes were aligned with these ambitious growth objectives.

The Challenge

The organization previously operated under a heavily regionalized, division-led approach. This strategy relied on local marketing to drive passenger numbers but failed to deliver sustained growth. Critical barriers included:

  • Organizational Silos: Data, content, marketing, and customer service operated in isolation, creating a disjointed view of the passenger.
  • Fragmented Ecosystem: Digital platforms prioritized internal structures over customer clarity.
  • Project-to-Project Mindset: A lack of centralized structure hindered continuous improvement and the effective use of data.
  • Customer Friction: Satisfaction was hampered by complex ticketing options and inconsistent scheduling frameworks.

Strategic Approach

Defining the future state

A central pillar of this project was the creation of a Future State Experience Map. We began by mapping current-state journeys for core customer archetypes, integrating cross-functional input and huge volumes of customer feedback from calls, emails, and social media.

We built a complete picture of the journey, including:

  • Underlying business processes.
  • Technology stacks and data usage.
  • Information and content needs at each stage.

Making the Vision Actionable

To sell this vision to the executive level, we created prototypes and storyboards. Not only to support the business case, but to prove that legacy design and customer experience challenges could be overcome. These weren't just visual concepts; they were validated from technical and operational perspectives to ensure they could work in real-world scenarios. Key solutions addressed:

  • Ticketing Clarity: Redesigning how regions present ticket options and promotional bundles.
  • Simplified journey planning: Using travel data to promote bus usage beyond the daily commute.
  • Real-Time Data: Incorporating traffic, passenger volume, and service delays to improve satisfaction.
  • 1-to-1 Personalisation: Demonstrating how customer data could be leveraged to enhance the passenger experience and provide moments of delight to build long term affinity.

Establishing Structural Requirements

I worked with data strategists and product management teams to draft the specifications for new digital products, which formed the basis of an RFP for new suppliers. Beyond the vision and design, I contributed to:

  • Content Architecture: Ensuring information was organized consistently across all regions.
  • Target Operating Model: Defining the roles and functions needed to manage the ecosystem centrally.

Impact

By focusing on structural foundations over surface-level design, we established a clear path toward the £80m revenue target.
 

  • Organisational: Aligned multiple business units around a single, customer-centric vision.
  • Strategic: Delivered the blueprint for future customer experience and digital investment.
  • Commercial: Established the models and experience tactics required to support aggressive growth.
