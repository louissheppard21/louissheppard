Selected case studies

Biffa. End-to-end transformation of the customer experience.

Biffa, a major UK waste utility provider, was embarking on a multi-year transformation to embed digital self-service across its SME and enterprise customer base. The goals were to drive revenue growth, improve customer satisfaction, and reduce reliance on manual service operations — fundamentally changing how the business delivered its service.

I led experience strategy and design teams across the programme, ensuring delivery stayed focused on research-driven customer outcomes aligned to commercial and operational goals.

The Challenge

Biffa’s existing service model relied heavily on phone-based sales and support, with fragmented systems and highly manual operational processes. This had created:

  • Long lead times to start or change a service
  • High operational cost and errors
  • Poor visibility for customers over contracts, collections and billing
  • Flatlining SME sales and high service desk dependency

At the same time, the space was heavily regulated and operationally risky — mistakes in pricing, service configuration or waste compliance could have serious financial and legal implications.

The challenge was to design simple, trustworthy self-serve journeys that could scale commercially without creating unacceptable business or compliance risk.

My Role

I lead design and product strategy across the programme, responsible for:

  • Setting the experience vision and long-term product direction
  • Leading and coaching a team of designers across multiple workstreams
  • Planning and running research to inform product strategy
  • Partnering with Product, Engineering and Delivery leads on roadmaps and trade-offs
  • Leading design teams through problem solving and framing opportunities
  • Acting as the interface between design teams and senior business stakeholders
  • Ensuring design decisions were grounded in operational reality and data availability

Discovery & Strategy

At the outset, I planned and led a comprehensive research programme combining:

  • Customer interviews across SME and enterprise segments
  • Quantitative surveys
  • Call listening sessions with sales and service desks
  • Stakeholder interviews across operations, compliance, finance and IT

This surfaced a set of consistent unmet needs:

  • Customers wanted to start, change and manage services online without waiting for callbacks
  • Price transparency and service configuration were confusing and error-prone
  • Stakeholders lacked confidence that changes made digitally would be executed correctly
  • Internally, teams lacked a shared view of the end-to-end customer journey

Using these insights, I led the creation of:

  • A future-state customer journey covering acquisition, onboarding, service management and billing
  • A modular experience framework to support very different customer types without fragmenting the product
  • A set of design principles balancing simplicity, trust and operational safety

These artefacts formed the foundation of the product vision and roadmap.

Key Product Decisions & Trade-offs

Rather than launching full self-serve for all customers at once, I recommended a phased approach, this was to help reduce regulatory and operational risk while showing early commercial value:

  • Phase 1: SME eCommerce onboarding
  • Phase 2: Core self-serve account management
  • Phase 3: Enterprise tooling and complex service changes

There was a need to serve highly diverse customer needs. We designed modular patterns that could adapt for different customer types without fragmenting the experience or adding unnecessary technical complexity, we designed for:

  • Small owner-operators
  • Multi-site enterprise customers
  • Broker ‘re-sellers’ of services
  • Different waste types and service frequencies

I prioritised service design ahead of detailed interface design, modelling backstage operations and data dependencies before finalising UI patterns. This ensured:

  • Interface designs were grounded in real operational constraints
  • We didn’t design journeys that the business couldn’t fulfil
  • Engineering teams could identify upstream system changes early

We encountered areas where significant complexity was slowing progress, for example for how to configure service contracting for larger site numbers or where there were additional regulatory considerations such as hazardous waste types.

I recommended deprioritizing these and focusing on more typical scenarios with higher customer volumes, and waiting to validate these with concrete quantitative data, then building in other journeys later on based on higher confidence that our core approach was working.

Delivery & Cross-functional Leadership

Working closely with Product, Engineering and Delivery leads, I:

  • Shaped a multi-year roadmap aligned to both commercial targets and operational readiness
  • Facilitated senior stakeholder workshops to align on scope, sequencing and risk appetite
  • Coached a team of designers across parallel workstreams, introducing shared rituals, critique sessions and review standards to maintain quality and consistency
  • Acted as a buffer between design teams and a highly complex stakeholder environment, allowing designers to stay focused on agreed outcomes while I managed competing business pressures
  • Stepped in hands-on to solve critical design challenges where they were blocking delivery, particularly around complex service configuration, pricing transparency and contract changes.

The Products

The programme delivered a connected ecosystem of products, including:

  • An SME eCommerce platform allowing customers to contract and start a waste service entirely online (an industry first)
  • A self-serve customer portal enabling all customer types to manage contracts, collections, invoices and service changes
  • Modular design system supporting consistent delivery across multiple product teams

Outcomes & Impact

  • 61% increase in SME sales, significantly exceeding targets
  • 38% reduction in service desk calls, lowering operational costs
  • Faster service onboarding times from 5 days to under 15 minutes with fewer manual processing errors.
  • A measurable cultural shift toward customer-centred decision making, driven by visible commercial impact

The solution enabled self-serve across contracting, onbooarding, and ongoing service management.

First Bus. Designing the future of travel for millions of customers.

First Bus was shifting from a business model focused primarily on operational efficiency toward one centred on revenue growth and customer experience. The business was planning major investment in technology and data, but lacked a coherent vision for how digital products, offline touchpoints, and operational change would work together to achieve its commercial goals.

I led experience and design strategy within a cross-functional team to define the long-term vision, shape the product roadmap, and support the business case for transformation.

The Challenge

First Bus’s customer experience was fragmented across multiple digital products, physical touchpoints, and regional operating models. Key issues included:

  • Inconsistent journey experiences across regions
  • Poor orchestration between digital and offline touchpoints
  • Limited personalisation and customer insight
  • Revenue opportunities being constrained by operational silos
  • No shared view of the end-to-end customer journey

At the same time, the organisation was preparing for a large-scale transformation investment, and senior leadership needed a credible, evidence-led vision to justify funding and align regional stakeholders.

My Role

I was responsible for:

  • Leading experience strategy and product vision development
  • Running discovery and research to inform strategic direction
  • Facilitating cross-functional alignment across product, engineering, data, and operations
  • Designing future-state journeys and ecosystem concepts
  • Supporting the business case for transformation at executive level

Discovery & Strategy

I led a structured discovery phase combining:

  • Stakeholder interviews across operations, regional leadership, marketing, data and IT
  • Review of customer research and service desk calls and social posts.
  • Current-state journey mapping to expose fragmentation and duplication
  • Working with engineering and data leads to identify data and integration gaps

This work surfaced a set of critical insights:

  • Customers struggled to navigate fragmented ticketing and account experiences
  • Lack of joined-up data limited personalisation and loyalty opportunities
  • Regional operating differences were preventing consistent product delivery
  • Revenue growth depended on better orchestration of digital and offline moments

Using these insights, I led the creation of:

  • A future-state end-to-end customer journey
  • An ecosystem vision showing how apps, websites, on-bus experiences and customer service would work together
  • A set of experience principles aligned to revenue and growth goals

These artefacts directly informed the strategic roadmap and business case.

Key Product Decisions

Rather than developing a roadmap based on individual product features, I advocated for designing the full end-to-end journey first. This allowed us to:

  • Identify the most commercially impactful moments
  • Ensure future work would not be duplicated across product teams
  • Plan a roadmap around customer value, not technical convenience

We developed frameworks that could support regional variation without fragmenting the product. This meant:

  • Standardising information architecture and data models
  • Allowing controlled variation for local needs
  • Avoiding a one-size-fits-all rollout that would fail regionally

I led cross functional design workshops then used these as a basis to develop high-fidelity journey concepts. These served to bring the overall vision to life and to unlock stakeholder buy-in with tangible outputs, rather than relying solely on strategy decks. These were data-enabled and technically validated ensuring they were feasible for future implementation.

Delivery & Cross-functional Leadership

Working closely with Product and Engineering leads, I:

  • Shaped a transformation roadmap aligned to commercial and operational goals
  • Facilitated co-design workshops with design, product, engineering and customer service teams
  • Mediated competing priorities between regional leadership and central product teams
  • Ensured design concepts were technically validated and operationally viable

Outcomes & Impact

  • Directly informed a multi-million-pound transformation investment now in delivery
  • Established a clear blueprint for digital product delivery and operational change
  • Aligned regional and central teams around a shared customer vision
  • Identified new revenue growth opportunities tied to loyalty and personalisation
  • Helped to shift leadership mindset from operational efficiency to experience-led growth

High fidelity designs made the strategic thinking tangible for all stakeholders and teams.

Speedy Hire. Design-led eCommerce and service transformation.

Speedy Hire, the UK’s largest equipment hire provider, was undertaking a multi-year programme to transform its eCommerce experience across web, app and point of sale. This was enabled by major investment in logistics, data and operational change, but early delivery lacked a unifying product vision.

I led product strategy and design teams across the programme, ensuring delivery stayed aligned to customer outcomes rather than short-term system constraints.

The Challenge

The existing eCommerce experience was built on legacy systems with a deeply flawed product taxonomy and fragmented user journeys. This resulted in:

  • Low product discoverability
  • High search failure rates
  • Confusing navigation for trade and retail customers
  • Critical early system decisions being made in isolation
  • Teams delivering features that compounded structural problems

Without intervention, the programme risked scaling a broken experience on top of new infrastructure.

My Role

I was responsible for:

  • Defining the long-term experience vision
  • Leading service design and journey mapping
  • Coaching design teams across web, app and POS
  • Partnering with Product and Engineering on roadmap sequencing
  • Advocating for structural fixes over superficial UI improvements
  • Establishing a scalable design system

Discovery & Strategy

I led a cross-functional discovery phase that included:

  • Current-state journey mapping workshops
  • User testing to identify navigation and discovery failures
  • Search analytics and keyword data analysis
  • Competitor benchmarking of taxonomy models

This revealed that:

  • The existing product taxonomy was fundamentally misaligned with customer intent
  • Search and navigation issues were structural, not cosmetic
  • Early platform decisions would lock in long-term failure if not corrected

Key Product Decisions & Trade-offs

I brought these findings to Product and Engineering leadership and made the case for rework despite timeline pressure, challenged the assumption that we should proceed with platform development before fixing the taxonomy. Instead, I proposed:

  • A full redesign of the product classification model
  • Alignment of taxonomy to customer mental models
  • Tree testing to validate findability improvements

This delayed some platform work but prevented long-term compounding failure. This required trading short-term delivery speed for long-term product viability.

I modelled user flows and backstage operations to surface:

  • Business rules adding unnecessary friction
  • Risk management constraints blocking single journeys
  • Opportunities to simplify account management and pricing logic

Working with product leadership we brought these findings to business leaders and used prototypes and user flows to model alternative journeys, helping to promote discussion and then change on underlying business rules.

Delivery & Cross-functional Leadership

Across the programme, I:

  • Coached designers embedded within delivery teams
  • Introduced shared critique and review standards
  • Mediated conflicts between delivery speed and product quality
  • Presented strategic trade-offs to senior stakeholders
  • Established a design system to ensure consistency at scale
  • Stepped in hands-on to prototype and test critical flows where they were blocking progress.

Outcomes & Impact

  • Prevented a flawed taxonomy from being locked into new infrastructure
  • Significantly improved product discoverability and reduced search failure in early testing.
  • Created a scalable design system adopted across teams
  • Helped to establish clearer customer account structures
  • Changed critical business rules while maintaining acceptable risk levels.
  • Established a clear, journey-led roadmap for future delivery

The programme is ongoing, with first major launch scheduled FY2026.
