Selected case studies

Biffa. Transforming the end-to-end customer experience, taking the business from flatlined sales to a 61% increase and driving an organisational shift to online self-serve.

Biffa, a major UK waste utility provider, was embarking on a multi-year transformation to embed digital self-service across its SME and enterprise customer base. The goals were to drive revenue growth, improve customer satisfaction, and reduce reliance on manual service operations — fundamentally changing how the business delivered its service across every stage of the customer journey.

 

I led experience strategy and design teams across the programme, ensuring delivery stayed focused on research-driven customer outcomes aligned to commercial and operational goals.

The Challenge

Biffa’s existing service model relied heavily on phone-based sales and support, with fragmented systems and manual operational processes. This had created long lead times to start a service, poor visibility for customers, high operational costs and flatlining SME sales.

The challenge was to design simple, trustworthy self-serve journeys that could scale commercially without creating unacceptable business or compliance risk in a highly regulated space.

My Role

I lead design and product strategy across the programme, responsible for:

  • Leading and coaching a team of designers across multiple workstreams, supporting problem solving and framing opportunities
  • Planning and running research to inform product strategy and gain ongoing feedback
  • Setting the experience vision and long-term product direction
  • Ideation and prototyping of early-stage concepts
  • Service design to map journeys, flows and connect interfaces to backstage systems
  • Ensuring design decisions were grounded in operational reality and data availability
  • Working with Product, Engineering and Delivery leads on roadmap and trade-offs discussions
  • Interfacing between design teams and a complex environment of senior business stakeholders
  • Design system oversight and cross-functional adoption

Research & Strategy

At the outset, I planned and led a research programme combining customer interviews across SME and enterprise segments, surveys, call listening with service desk teams, and business stakeholder interviews.

While needs uncovered across the customer segments were diverse, there were common themes. Customers wanted a service that felt invisible, any disruption would draw focus, framing the relationship around infrequent negative moments.

Poor communication and visibility would compound this, with business process inadvertently drawing customers to further fixate on issues. We uncovered many misplaced assumptions around waste treatment and environmental impact, especially within the SME segment.

Incorrect product configuration was common for SME customers and this resulted in charges for the customer and re-contracting, getting new relationships off to a bad start.

There was a clear opportunity to reframe the relationship around more positive narratives and de-escalate issues through improved communication and resolution approach. I tied insights to product recommendations and developed an experience strategy to playback to the business. After securing executive buy-in I worked with product teams to build out a multi-year roadmap and delivery plan.

Key Product Decisions

To reduce regulatory and operational risk and see early commercial value we planned a phased approach:

  • Phase 1: SME eCommerce onboarding
  • Phase 2: Core self-serve account management
  • Phase 3: Enterprise tooling and complex service changes

The solution needed to support highly diverse customer needs, from single service and site all the way up to complex service estates, to manage this I developed a design system and modular framework, with logic to adapt interfaces based on the scale of information. This enabled tailored experiences without introducing excessive technical complexity.

Customers wanted better visibility, but gaps and latency in data made this challenging. I advocated for an approach to always display an update or offer an action even when the full detail may not yet be available, we created messaging, statuses and notifications and mapped these to existing data points to achieve this and bridge the gap.

A key insight was the need to better communicate environmental impact to all customer types. As well as detailed reporting views to serve enterprise needs, we developed simplified insights and content that would resonate with SME customers and drive consideration of how product choices and their own waste handling effected carbon emissions. Beyond the digital product experience this approach was adopted more widely within the business influencing content and marketing strategy.

Design Approach

At the beginning of each phase I prioritised service design ahead of detailed UI, using cross-functional workshops to model a view of the journey with backstage operations and data dependencies. This ensured interface designs were grounded in operational constraints and we didn’t design journeys that the business couldn’t fulfil. It also gave engineering teams the opportunity to identify upstream system changes early and build alignment across teams ahead of further delivery.

In each phase key flows and features were prototyped upfront, then validated by engineering and tested with customers. I was also able to use these to communicate direction of travel back to the wider business, offering reassurance on feasibility and commercial viability, and assessing appetite for risk.

Through the program I used regular design reviews for teams to share progress and thinking, scale the design system, and ensure design principles and strategic needs were being adopted and interpreted correctly. These also enabled me to identify were teams needed support to unblock challenges.

Outcomes

  • Took flatlined SME sales to 61% YoY increase, significantly exceeding targets
  • 38% reduction in service desk calls, lowering operational costs
  • Faster service onboarding times from 5 days to under 15 minutes with fewer manual processing errors.
  • A measurable cultural shift toward customer-centred decision making, driven by visible commercial impact
  • Established an embedded test and learn optimisation approach and team to drive ongoing improvement

The Open. A digital ecosystem for the iconic golf championship to drive monetization and fandom, increasing digital revenue by 78% and selling out the event for the first time in it’s 150 year history.

In an increasingly competitive landscape, The Open had identified the use of digital as critical to grow it’s fanbase, increase engagement and drive commercial performance. I led strategy and design for a multi-year program to create a new fan engagement ecosystem spanning marketing, ticket sales, year-round build up and live event coverage.

The Challenge

Despite its preeminent position in global sports The Open was facing critical challenges, rival sports brands were investing heavily in audience acquisition and there was an awareness that their legacy fanbase would not sustain them into the future in a rapidly evolving landscape. Their own market research had highlighted a perceived barrier to engage with the game by potential new audiences.

Monetizing their existing audience was also becoming harder as a proliferation of other platforms offered coverage of their event and were competing for their audience share.

They had developed a 5-year plan to address this with a central objective to sell out The Open for the first time in the events history. The use digital had been identified as central to achieving this, as well as growing their global audience and driving much stronger commercial focus.

The challenge was to help deliver this ambition while working to immoveable ticket release and event windows.

My Role

I lead design and product strategy across the programme, responsible for:

  • Defining the experience strategy and product principles
  • Leading research activities and aligning cross-functional stakeholders
  • Leading design direction through multiple phases covering ticketing, year-round build up and live coverage across web and app
  • Leading and coaching a team of designers, supporting problem solving and framing opportunities
  • Hands-on execution to design journey flows, interface concepts, backstage logic and detailed interaction design for key features
  • Design system implementation and oversight
  • Planning and running user testing activity
  • Supporting prioritisation and sequencing against immovable tournament deadlines

Research & Strategy

The Open had invested in market research and developed target segmentation and personas, these gave us a basis for understanding of the needs and challenges for engaging each audience type.

My team and I attended The Open to embed within their teams and understand how the event was being delivered at an operational level, this included:

  • Spending time with content and journalism teams within the media centre
  • Course walkthroughs with data and scoring teams
  • Meeting with media and sponsorship partners
  • Speaking directly with attending fans

In addition to this I reviewed analytics across the event days and through year-round build up to understand how engagement and performance tracked against key milestones, through qualification and build up, ticket sales, and the championship itself.

Key Product Decisions

The existing ticket sales journey experienced high abandonment at all stages. I developed an approach to present stronger narratives around the tournament days, utilising highlights and archive content to build anticipation and stronger reasons to consider purchase, then developed a custom ticket builder journey to simplify purchase.

To broaden accessibility and address the need to engage a wider audience, I developed the concept of two layers within the experience, an ‘interest layer’ where information was simplified, immediate and quick to digest and then an ‘insight layer’ that could be triggered for optional depth. This balanced the needs of legacy fans with the ambition to make the game more widely accessible.

Working with engineering I created an underlying automation logic based on key events within the Championship to adapt the experience or surface content. This removed the need for continuous manual reconfiguration and ensured the experience kept pace with the on-course action.

Data review had shown that website visitors typically dwelt on the leaderboard and much of the data, editorial and video on offer lacked engagement. I created interaction principles that made discovery intuitive, with fans able to trigger quick access to related content from any point, and more eaily take deeper journeys into the experience.

I designed a custom publishing interface for content teams to rapidly create short form stories and content snippets. This enabled them to provide broader and better coverage of the multiple narratives that developed across the tournament days, and give fans a far richer commentary experience.

Design Approach

Delivery was sequenced around three phases aligned to immovable dates, ahead of each phase I ran co-design workshops with cross-functional teams to uncover requirements and shape early solution concepts. These were framed by highlighting problem statements and opportunities uncovered in early research.

Outputs were turned quickly into interactive prototypes that we could test with fans and use to get stakeholder buy-in ahead of detailed UI, this also gave engineering and product teams visibility of early thinking and direction of travel.

We also ran user testing in later stages to refine interactions and language, this de-risked delivery especially around the short ticket release window and the championship, where there would be no opportunity for meaningful test and learn when the product waslive.

I developed a visual direction to present an evolution of The Open brand, rooted in it’s heritage but positioning it more assertively as future-facing and purposefully distinct from the digital products of other golf championships. This was driven by the insight that many casual fans found golf impenetrable or even elitist, we wanted our experience to challenge this assumption and offer something exhilarating and unexpected.

Outcomes

  • On launch the ticketing solution sold out the event for the first time in 150 years (5 years ahead of target)
  • Introduced waiting list and early bird prioritisation for the following event
  • +76% increase in attributable digital revenue during the live event
  • 250,000 new membership sign-ups within the first event
  • +56% increase across combined engagement metrics

First Bus. Designing the future of travel for millions of customers, guiding a major, multi-year program to drive new revenue and build long-term loyalty.

First Bus was shifting from a business model focused primarily on operational efficiency toward one centred on revenue growth and customer experience. The business was planning major investment in technology and data, but lacked a coherent vision for how digital products, offline touchpoints, and operational change would work together to achieve its commercial goals.

I led experience and design strategy within a cross-functional team to define the long-term vision, shape the product roadmap, and support the business case for transformation.

The Challenge

First Bus’s customer experience was fragmented by a highly regionalised approach with little overarching governance of the customer experience or ability to build data insight. This was constraining revenue opportunities and hampering efforts internally to build momentum and buy-in for the planned transformation.

The challenge was to show a credible, evidence-led vision that would secure executive approval, achieve buy-in of regional leads, and then to set out an actionable plan for delivery.

My Role

  • Leading experience strategy and design concept development
  • Running discovery and research to inform strategic direction
  • Facilitating cross-functional alignment across product, engineering, data, and operations
  • Designing future-state journeys and ecosystem concepts
  • Supporting the business case for transformation at executive level

Research & Strategy

Within a cross-functional team I led customer experience research, consulting with regional leadership, marketing, and customer service teams, reviewing customer feedback, and supporting wider conversations with data and IT teams.

I facilitated workshops to map the current state journey and worked with product, data and engineering leads to identify integration gaps. This exposed fragmentation within the journey, with major differences between web, app and physical touch-points, confusion over ticketing and prices, and little opportunity to capture data or offer personalised experiences.

I developed a future-state journey for all core customer types, this provided a complete picture of how interconnected products and off-line touch points would be orchestrated to drive the revenue targets the business had set, and then established with cross-functional leads what technology, data and content were needed at each stage.

Based on this we created a number of strategic assets for guiding the implementation, this included a content architecture, reporting model, target operating model, customer data strategy, and a product roadmap.

Design Approach

To sell the vision into regional and executive leadership I created high-fidelity prototypes and storyboards that made the journey and commercial opportunity tangible for stakeholders. These also provided the opportunity to solve for long standing experience challenges and generate solution concepts for enhancing loyalty through better use of customer data.

I ran co-creation workshops with design, product and engineering teams around key moments of the journey and then led design of the concepts based on these. The concepts were then technically validated for future implementation and presented across the business helping to unlock buy-in for the transformation.

Outcomes & Impact

  • Directly informed a multi-million-pound transformation investment now in delivery
  • Established a clear blueprint for digital product delivery and operational change
  • Aligned regional and central teams around a shared customer vision
  • Identified new revenue growth opportunities tied to loyalty and personalisation
  • Helped to shift leadership mindset from operational efficiency to experience-led growth

01/01
Future-state journey mapping.

Speedy Hire. eCommerce and service transformation across a product ecosystem, re-designing business processes, foundational systems and customer experiences.

Speedy Hire, the UK’s largest equipment hire provider, are undertaking a multi-year programme to transform its eCommerce experience across web, app and point of sale. This is enabled by major investment in logistics, data and operational change, but early delivery lacked a unifying product vision.

 

I led product strategy and design teams across the programme, to ensure delivery stayed aligned to customer outcomes rather than short-term system constraints.

The Challenge

The existing eCommerce experience was built on legacy systems with a deeply flawed product taxonomy and fragmented user journeys. There were critical issues across all stages of the journey, especially within product search and purchase flows, driven not only by UI but by underlying business rules.

At the outset it was clear product and engineering teams lacked an overarching view of how efforts would align to drive customer outcomes, with early systems decisions being made in isolation and critical risks that continuing delivery would compound existing structural problems.

My Role

  • Leading and coaching design teams across development of a product ecosystem (web, app and POS)
  • Service design to map current journey, processes and pain points, and establish future-state
  • Advocating for customer outcomes and structural fixes over superficial UI improvements
  • Collaborating with product and engineering on roadmap and delivery sequencing
  • Planning and running research to inform product strategy and gaining ongoing feedback
  • Leading the redesign of the product catalogue taxonomy utilising keyword and sales data, AI modelling and tree testing
  • Prototyping, user flow modelling and workshop facilitation to highlight and drive change of underlying business process and rules
  • Design system authorship, oversight and cross-functional adoption

Research & Strategy

I led research which included workshops to define the current state customer journey, business process and technical dependencies, user testing and data analysis of current state digital experience, and business stakeholder consultation.

This revealed that existing product taxonomy was fundamentally misaligned with customer intent and that search and navigation issues were structural, not cosmetic. With early platform work already underway there was a critical risk that long-term failure would be locked in.

Previous M&A activity had also resulted in a disparate service offer, the business was investing in brand development to support a clearer articulation of their offer, but this needed to be delivered through a compete rethink of how the service was delivered digitally.

I produced a product vision and experience principles to set the direction of future work and developed a future-state customer journey map to provide alignment to all teams around how work-streams would align for customer outcomes. This enabled us to sequence delivery around early value and set clear boundaries for product areas.

Key Product Decisions

I brought taxonomy findings to Product and Engineering leadership and made the case for rework despite timeline pressure and challenged the assumption that we should proceed with platform development before fixing the taxonomy. Instead, I proposed a full redesign of the product classification model to align to customer intent, and tree testing to validate. This required trading short-term delivery speed for long-term product viability.

Purchase journeys and account creation options were confusing for customers, with rules and conditions around fraud prevention, late charges, and return options poorly communicated and blocking progress. I modelled user flows to surface where this was adding friction and working with product leadership brought these findings to business leaders. I used prototypes and user flows to model alternative journeys, helping to promote discussion and then change on underlying business rules.

Aside from better serving their core trade and enterprise customers, the business wanted to increase it’s share of the home DIY segment. Consultations with these customers highlighted they struggled to match suitable products to their projects, we developed new categorisations and tagging to enable these customers to more easily understand product suitability for their skill level and type of project.

Design Approach

With an overarching customer journey map in place with key requirements and customer needs mapped to this, I was able to organise design teams around defined product areas. I established design principles and used regular reviews to share progress and learnings across teams.

With all digital products being re-built and an updated brand in development there was an opportunity to establish a new design system. We developed conceptual designs for several key pages to give us the sense of how visual direction should be shaped against brand needs and experience aims, and used this to build out an initial set of components and foundations to build upon.

This ensured all teams started off the same footing and with a shared understanding, I then used regular cross functional reviews for us to agree how to scale the system based on decisions being made within interaction and journey design, and discuss how we wanted to evolve and strengthen guidance over time. Later establishing a governance model for ongoing contributions and maintenance.

Working with product leads I developed a KPI tree mapping measurement metrics to wider organisational goals. Within design I ensured that all work was tracked against a specific KPI and there was continuous visibility of where and how design activity was supporting the strategic aims of the business.

Outcomes & Impact

    The programme is ongoing, with first major launch scheduled for FY2026 however it has already delivered tangible value.

  • Prevented a flawed taxonomy from being locked into new infrastructure
  • Significantly improved product discoverability and reduced search failure in early testing.
  • Created a scalable design system adopted across teams
  • Helped to establish clearer customer account structures
  • Changed critical business rules while maintaining acceptable risk levels.
  • Established a clear, journey-led roadmap for future delivery
